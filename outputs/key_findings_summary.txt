
GCC TRADE FLOWS: KEY ANALYTICAL FINDINGS (2015-2023)
Data: World Bank WITS / UN Comtrade / IMF DOTS

FINDING 1 - UAE IS THE MOST DIVERSIFIED GCC ECONOMY BY FAR
  Oil % of exports in 2023: UAE 12.2% vs Kuwait 91.6% vs Qatar 88.2%
  UAE non-oil exports reached $520B in 2023 - driven by re-exports,
  financial services, tourism and manufacturing.
  The UAE proves that GCC diversification is achievable at scale.

FINDING 2 - SAUDI ARABIA'S VISION 2030 SHOWING EARLY RESULTS
  Non-oil exports grew from $30.4B (2015) to $65B (2023) - a 114% increase.
  Non-oil GDP share rose from 41% to 52% over the same period.
  However, oil still represents 80% of export value - transition ongoing.

FINDING 3 - ALL GCC ECONOMIES ARE HIGHLY CHINA-DEPENDENT FOR TRADE
  China is the top export partner for Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Oman.
  India is UAE's top partner reflecting re-export and diaspora trade.
  This concentration creates geopolitical trade risk.

FINDING 4 - 2020 WAS THE STRESS TEST - UAE RECOVERED FASTEST
  COVID + oil price collapse hit all GCC nations.
  UAE exports fell only 18% vs Saudi Arabia's 34% decline.
  UAE's diversified base provided structural resilience.

FINDING 5 - 2022 ENERGY PRICE SPIKE MASKED DIVERSIFICATION PROGRESS
  GCC total exports hit $1,590B in 2022 - an all-time record.
  But this was driven entirely by elevated oil prices, not structural change.
  Real diversification progress is visible only in non-oil export data.
